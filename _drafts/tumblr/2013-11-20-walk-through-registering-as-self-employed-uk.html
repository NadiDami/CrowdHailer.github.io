Today I registered myself as self-employed. 

I have been self employed for a number of months, working as a personal tutor for maths and sciences in London. I have been keeping accurate records of all the hours I work in a notepad for two reasons.

  1. For my own interest to work out if my self employed status is sustainable.
  2. Because I know somewhere along the line I will need these records to placate the tax man.

There are many advantages to being self employed and an option I think more people should consider. A large factor in dissuading people is taking full responsibility for their administration. I have been putting it off myself but today I sort it out.

NOTE: this information is for the UK only, not conclusive and may quickly be out of date

Check that you are self-employed

First on the  HM Revenue & Customs list, this I assume this would be obvious in the vast majority of cases. For completeness here are the situations that make you self employed in the eyes of our government. Answer yes to any of these?

  1. Do you have the final say in how the business is run?
  2. Are you responsible for meeting any losses as well as taking any profits?
  3. Can you hire someone on your own terms to do work for you?
  4. Do you risk your own money?
  5. Do you provide the main items of equipment you need to do your job?
  6. Do you agree to do a job for a fixed price regardless of how long it may take?
  7. Can you decide what work to do, how and when to work and where to provide the services?
  8. Do you regularly work for a number of different people?
  9. Do you have to correct unsatisfactory work in your own time and at your own expense?

So with my answer yes to all of these but #6 I can conclude that I definitely am self-employed. 

Registering for Self Assessment

Before I can fill in a tax return I must first register as self employed.  

When to register?

Copied direct from HMRC.

“It’s best if you register with HMRC as soon as your circumstances change. The latest you should register is by 5 October after the end of the tax year for which you need a tax return. The tax year runs from 6 April one year to 5 April the next.”

Information needed to register?

Registering for self assessment for the first time.

  • National Insurance Number.
  • Contact Details.
  • Date your circumstances changed.

Fortunately I have all of these so on to registering… 

Registering

So registering as self assessed for the first time I have to choose this option.

  • I want to tell HMRC that I am in business and need to register for a new tax or tell HMRC that the company is now active

I did not realise that I qualified as a company being a self employed individual but this seams to be the case.

Before I can register I have to sign up for a Government Gateway account, that bit at least  was easy to do.

Which taxes to pay?

Next up, chooseing which tax(es) you need to sign up for. The options are any of these four

  • Corporation Tax (if the company is registered with Companies House)
  • Self Assessment (including Class 2 National Insurance contributions)
  • PAYE for Employers (if you have employees)
  • VAT (if business exceeds VAT threshold, currently sales £79,000)

With a low income and no employees I conclude that the only one pertaining to my situation is Self Assessment. 

Applying

So after all of that I know get to apply. They ask for all the standard personal information. Happily much less than I thought they could ask taking only a few minutes to fill in.
